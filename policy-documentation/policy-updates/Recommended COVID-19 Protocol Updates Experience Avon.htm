Recommended COVID-19 Protocol Updates
Aug 15 2021

Avon families, 

Good evening. As I explained in my ParentSquare message last week, I met on Friday with the Hendricks County Health Department and Superintendents from other school corporations in our county. The purpose of this letter is to update you on the changes I will recommend to our existing COVID-19 protocols and the timeline for implementation. These changes are necessary due to a significant increase in positive cases last week and hundreds of students quarantined as a result. Additional data and rationale for this recommendation is provided in full below.

Recommendations 

Based on current data, public health recommendations, and my resolve to keep students and staff healthy and in school, I will be making the following recommendations to the Avon Board of School Trustees: 

  1. Masks are required in all elementary and intermediate schools for students and adults. 
  2. Limited access of guests and volunteers in schools while students are present. 
  3. Desks/tables spaced out in classrooms as much as possible. 
  4. By requiring masks in those schools, the quarantine radius is reduced from 6 feet to 3 feet in classroom settings. 

Avon Schools Data  
The number of positive cases in elementary schools last week exceeds our highest numbers of any week last school year. Last week alone, there were 25 positive elementary cases. The highest number last year was 17 in a single week. At intermediate schools this week, we saw positive case counts nearly equal to our highest case counts from last year. While Avon Middle School North and Avon High School are not seeing significant increases, Avon Middle School South is also experiencing high counts. Since K-6 students are not eligible for a vaccination, I am suggesting that we make masking changes at those schools and not in middle and high school at this time. Masks continue to be strongly recommended at those schools. Masks are also strongly recommended at preschool at this time, but not required due to low positive cases and the challenges of requiring 3- and 4-year-old students to wear masks properly.   

An equally important factor leading to this recommendation is the high number of quarantined students required by the Countyâ€™s current quarantine guidelines. These guidelines are having a profoundly negative effect on student learning. Over the first two and a half weeks of school, 84 individuals have tested positive for COVID-19, and we have quarantined almost 800 students or 8% of all Avon students.  In the first 12 days of the 2021-22 school year, students have lost 4,030 days of instruction to quarantines. This cannot continue! Our School Board will meet early this week in Executive Session to weigh immediate changes to quarantine protocols. While masking students and adults in the elementaries and intermediate schools is an action neither I nor our board want to take, it is evident that this step is necessary to reduce quarantines. It is my hope that, if approved by the School Board, these revised protocols will only need to be in place while we remain Orange as a county and that we can return to the protocols currently in place once we are back to Yellow. 

Next Steps 
I have asked our School Board to evaluate these recommendations and have scheduled a School Board meeting on Wednesday, August 18, at 7:00 pm. To allow for social distancing, this meeting will take place in the Auditorium at Avon Middle School South. 

This meeting is open to the public, however due to the time sensitivity of these decisions, there will not be an opportunity for public comment. We have received scores of phone calls and emails advocating both for and against masks. In addition, we have heard comments from attendees at recent Board Meetings. I and our board appreciate all those who take time to respectfully provide their thoughts and opinions. We have listened closely, and we are grateful that Avon families, students, staff, and community members care deeply about our schools and our community. The Boardâ€™s decision will be based upon a review of data from our schools and collaboration with the health department, not on the number of people who speak for or against masks at this meeting. You are welcome to email me and the School Board prior to this meeting. Our email addresses can be found on our website.

I will communicate with you again on Wednesday evening once the School Board has considered these recommendations.  

Sincerely,

Scott M. Wyndham, Ed.D. 
Superintendent
