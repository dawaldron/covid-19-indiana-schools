3 Hamilton County school districts now require students and staff to wear masks

Original article: Carmel Clay, Hamilton Southeastern and Noblesville schools are making masks required starting Monday, the districts announced on Friday.

These are the first mask mandates in Hamilton County schools this school year and are happening as students have had just two weeks of class or less.

Each of the three districts said they have positive cases as well as hundreds of students in quarantine since school started, which was Aug. 2 in Noblesville, Aug. 4 at HSE and Thursday at Carmel.

In emails sent to families late Friday afternoon, the districts said the mask mandates will be for students and staff regardless of vaccination status starting Monday.

Districts pointed to those increasing cases and quarantines as well as guidance from health officials as to the reasoning for the changes.

The new mandates mean that Hamilton County’s school districts, which previously recommended masks, are now split: three districts have mandates and three – Hamilton Heights, Sheridan and Westfield Washington – do not.

While the topic of masks in schools has been debated by parents throughout the pandemic, the back-and-forth has grown as this school year approached and started.

Federal, state and local health officials have recommended that schools adopt universal masking, but have stopped short of mandates for this year.

The state previously had a mask mandate for schools, but starting July 1, it became a decision that was up to local school boards, who in some cases designated their superintendent to decide.

And that put school leaders, who are not health officials, in a difficult position as they listened to experts but also to the outcry from parents both opposed and in favor of masks.

Plus, the start of this school has looked very different from last year, with schools returning to full-time, in-person learning as the more contagious delta variant spreads.

Cases rising and hundreds of students in quarantine

In their messages to families, HSE and Carmel acknowledged that tension, saying they know there will be opposition, but masks will allow districts to reduce quarantine numbers. 

“I know this will not make everyone happy, but it is my goal to protect our students and staff and keep our schools open and running,” HSE Superintendent Yvonne Stokes said in her message to parents. 

The districts' emails also shared data about cases and quarantines so far. 

This week in Noblesville, hundreds of students were quarantined including roughly 200 at Noblesville East Middle School and a classroom of students at White River Elementary as well as other students.

The COVID rates are already at the peak of what they were last winter and student absenteeism rates are the highest they’ve been during the pandemic, per the district.

At HSE, which started classes on Aug. 4, there have been 80 positive cases among students and more than 500 students deemed to be close contacts.

And at Carmel, which has had two days of class so far, cases are rising and hundreds of students quarantined.

Despite these changes, the other districts in Hamilton County are sticking with their recommendations.

Westfield also sent a message to families on Friday that said masks will continue to be “optional but strongly recommended” for everyone.

The district pointed out that Hamilton County is currently yellow on the Indiana State Department of Health’s COVID-19 map and “if we move to orange, we will reconsider our mask policy.” That map is updated weekly at noon on Wednesday.

Sheridan and Heights will also continue to recommend masks and are monitoring local data, the districts confirmed.

Earlier this week, on Thursday, Indiana had more than 3,000 new cases – the highest one-day total since January.
