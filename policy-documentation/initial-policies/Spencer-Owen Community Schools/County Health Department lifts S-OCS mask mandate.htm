County Health Department lifts S-OCS mask mandate

Masks now optional inside S-OCS buildings

Amanda York
Evening World

In a decision posted to the Owen County Health Department's Facebook page Monday after, county health officer, Dr. Diane Wells, has chosen to eliminate the mask mandate for Spencer-Owen Schools at this time

The post reads:

"Life is too short and sometimes you have to pick your battles. Our staff is too small to continue to fight some battles. COVID Response 8-16-21

"1. When concerns regarding a mask mandate were expressed to me, I was not aware that the school board had unanimously declined that option and to my chagrin, I did not make the effort to find that out and did not have that data to factor into my decision. That oversight is mine. I express my regret to the school board as a duly elected administrative body and offer an apology to each board member for any appearance of disrespect, none was intended.

"2. Regarding the Covid/Mask discussion there is copious information and multiple interpretations; the Indiana Department of Health does recommend a mask for schools. The temporary mask mandate was intended to limit school place exposure in August and reduce contact quarantines. So far masking has reduced the number of students traced by ½ according to school nurses.

"3. However, the benefits of the mandate seem to be outweighed by negative consequences. Necessary health department functions are delayed by excess calls and emails. The passion and anger of Owen County residents is acknowledged with respect and an intent that it stays with me and not be reflected or focused elsewhere. I apologize for any actions which apparently contributed to the need for some to attack me personally; I wish for them and for all our community a willingness to act and react from a place of healing, health, and forgiveness.

"4. I want to applaud and thank the staff of the Health Department and the School Nurse team for their dedication throughout this process. Testing and vaccinations will continue as scheduled. In the school contact tracing and quarantines will be according to state guidelines.

"5. School has been opened for 2 weeks and the period of contagion for those returning from summer travel is passed. Therefore, it is my impression that in this county the greater good will be served by resuming the mask option previously approved by the school board.

"6. I will continue to work for optimal public health in the community. God’s blessings to all.

"Respectfully, Diane Wells, M.D."

The announcement was met with mixed reaction via social media comments on the post with several thanking Dr. Wells for doing what she thinks is right to help our community.
