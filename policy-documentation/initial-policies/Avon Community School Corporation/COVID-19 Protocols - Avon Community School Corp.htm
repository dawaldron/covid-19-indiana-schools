COVID-19 Protocols

2021-22 COVID-19 Protocols by KevinCarr

August 12 update - How can my child connect with teachers to complete assignments during quarantine?

Students will continue to work with their teacher on Schoology to complete work while they are at home. Students can access Schoology via the Students page on our website.

August 2 update - If my student has tested positive for COVID in the past 90 days do they need to quarantine?

If a student has tested positive in the past 90 days they are exempt from quarantine guidelines. However, the State Department of Health wants all close contacts to be notified, even if they are exempt from quarantine. When you are notified, please reach out to your school nurse or contact the COVID phone to confirm when your student tested positive and to confirm it is documented that they are exempt from quarantine. 

July 28 update - What changed because of the CDC's July 27 update?

The only change is that now masks are recommended for vaccinated individuals as well as for unvaccinated individuals.

July 28 update - If I send my child to school without a mask, will they be required to wear one? 

While local health officials and Avon Schools recommend masks while in our schools, there is currently no mask requirement. The only exception is on buses as per the federal mandate. 

July 28 update - What if my child loses or forgets their mask?

Extra masks will be available on buses and at schools. In order to sustain our supply, please plan to send your child to school with their own mask if you want them to wear one. 

Will we continue to receive parent notifications of positive cases and close contacts?

Yes.  ACSC’s contact tracing team will make notifications prior to 5:00 pm during the week and on Sundays from 12:00 pm to 5:00 pm. All parent communications related to positive cases and/or close contacts will be done via ParentSquare text/email notification.

If my child is told to quarantine, but is vaccinated, what are my options?

As long as your child remains asymptomatic, you have three options: 

#1 – Your child can complete the quarantine as directed and return after 10-day quarantine. 

#2 – Your child can return on day 8 with a negative PCR test result administered during days 5-7 post exposure or a rapid test administered on day 8.  No at-home COVID-19 tests will be accepted.   

#3 – Your child can return immediately if you provide proof of full vaccination (15 days after second shot) to the school nurse.  If your child is told to quarantine multiple times, this may be requested each time.     

Regardless of which option a parent selects, you will receive communication from the State Department of Health as a result of being identified as a close contact.

If my child has symptoms, can I get them back to school with a negative rapid test?

Yes, once you have shared rapid test results with the school nurse.  In-home tests are not accepted. 

Can I request a mask-required classroom or teacher for my child?

No.  We will not be separating classes based on mask status.

Are there locations where a mask will be required?

Yes.  Federal requirements indicate a mask must be worn on public transportation.

Vaccines

PCR Testing

In early February, Indiana updated guidance for students quarantined due to close contacts with COVID+ individuals. Now, students may return to school on day 8 after exposure to a COVID+ individual if they (1) remain symptom-free and (2) provide proof of a negative PCR test taken on or after day 5-7 past exposure to their school nurse. See below or reference the ParentSquare message you received after you were notified of the close contact for guidance on dates that apply to your student's exposure.

This has prompted questions about the types of COVID-19 tests, where to access testing, and how long students must quarantine depending on the type of test they were administered.

Types of Tests

There are two primary types of COVID-19 tests, antigen tests (commonly referred to as ‘rapid tests’) and Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) tests. They are compared and contrasted in the following table.

Antigen/Rapid Test PCR Test Antibody Test
  • Collected with a nasal or throat swab
  • Detects a protein that is part of COVID-19
  • Useful for identifying people at or near peak infection
  • Generally faster - 15-16 minutes
  • Can be less accurate - May give false negatives (when people are past peak but still contagious
  • Collected with nasal or throat swab
  • Detects genetic material of COVID-19
  • Useful for identifying people before, during, or after peak infection
  • Generally slower - 1-7 days
  • Close to 100% accurate - 'Gold Standard' - Unlikely to give false negatives (able to detect trace amounts of virus
 Antibody tests are not diagnostic in nature and cannot be used to determine if someone has COVID-19 and is able to spread the virus.

Adapted from UC Davis Health, Different types of COVID-19 tests explained.

Where to Access Testing

There is one location in Hendricks County where free PCR testing is available:

Danville Site

202 Meadow Dr.
Danville, IN 46122                             

>> Schedule a test

 

Individuals may also reach out to the following resources:

  • Visit ourshot.in.gov for local vaccine clinics
  • Visit scheduling.coronavirus.in.gov for local testing sites
  • Call 211
  • Reach out to your local pharmacy

When scheduling, click ‘I don’t have a registration code’ and choose an appointment time. Testing is free for all Hoosiers. Bring a state-issued ID, work ID, or utility bill to prove residency. When you arrive, it may be a good idea to clarify you are there to receive a PCR test.

If desired, you can also schedule PCR tests at CVS and Walgreens.

Quarantine Guidelines

Students must meet these two criteria: (1) They must remain symptom-free and (2) they must provide proof of a negative PCR test taken on or after day 5-7 past exposure to their school nurse. If they meet these two criteria, they may return to school on day 8 past exposure.

This calendar shows an example timeline for a student who is deemed to be in close contact with a COVID+ individual on February 1. Because this student has remained symptom-free, his parent/caregiver schedules him to receive a PCR test on the earliest day possible, Saturday, February 6. Notification of a negative result is delivered to the family on Monday, February 8, and the student may return to school on the following day, Tuesday, February 9. 

 

NOTE: The Hendricks County Health Department is the final authority on when a student may return to school. So, the above is subject to change based upon their guidance.
