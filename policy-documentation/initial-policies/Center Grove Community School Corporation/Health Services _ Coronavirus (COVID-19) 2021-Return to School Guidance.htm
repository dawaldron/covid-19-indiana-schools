  •  *Updated August 4, 2021

  • STAY HOME WHEN SICK - WASH HANDS OFTEN

    FACE COVERINGS 

    OPTIONAL for students and staff under most circumstances, including classrooms, hallways, cafeterias, and athletic facilities. May be required in clinics and for those quarantined as close contacts who wish to return to school on days 8-14. REQUIRED BY FEDERAL MANDATE for students and staff on school buses. Students who do have a face covering may request one from the driver, but will not be turned away from the school bus.

    SOCIAL DISTANCING 

    Classrooms to observe 3' of social distancing when possible. Elementary cafeterias may reduce social distancing with a goal of 3' distance where possible. Secondary will return to normal with distancing to 3' when possible, 6' spacing when possible for activities with increased exhalation.

     VOLUNTEERS/VISITORS/RENTALS

    Parents/guardians will be allowed to volunteer when vaccinated or wearing face coverings. Visitors will be allowed for events outside the school day. Lunch-time visitors will not be allowed. Building rentals will resume. All visitors should either be vaccinated or wear a mask while in the building.

    ENHANCED CLEANING

    Enhanced 3-step cleaning practices will continue in all school buildings and on school buses with special attention to high-touch areas, including water fountains and restrooms.
     

    EXTRA/CO-CURRICULAR ACTIVITIES

    Extra-curricular and co-curricular activities may return to normal with additional safety measures as required for participants. 

     

    CONTACT TRACING/QUARANTINES

    Positive cases and symptomatic close contacts will be required to isolate/quarantine as required by the IDH and may return per CGCSC Quarantine Guidelines.  

     

  • STUDENT QUARANTINES

    Per Indiana Code (410 IAC 1-2.5), ALL individuals identified as close contacts MUST be quarantined from school.

    POSITIVE CASES

    • Positive cases (symptomatic): Must isolate for 10 days from symptom onset.
    • Positive cases (asymptomatic): Must isolate for 10 days from the date of the test.

    CLOSE CONTACTS

    Quarantine length is 14 days from the date of exposure

    • The Johnson County Health Department defines a close contact as an individual within 3’ or less for at least 15 minutes regardless of the wearing of masks. 
    • Cases of physical contact, such as athletics or extra/co-curricular activities, will be handled on a case-by-case basis.

  • RETURNING FROM QUARANTINE
    Center Grove will allow three (3) options to return from quarantine if asymptomatic.

    All individuals identified as a close contact will not have to quarantine if they:

    1. Provide proof of being fully vaccinated; OR
    2. Provide proof of an independent positive COVID test within the past 6 months; OR 
    3. Provide proof of an antibody test within the past 6 months.
