Richmond Schools will require masks for staff, students regardless of vaccination status

Gus Martin

RICHMOND, Ind.  Masks will be required in all Richmond Community Schools buildings regardless of vaccination status starting Monday, Aug. 9.

RCS Superintendent Curtis Wright issued a letter to the Red Devil community on Friday explaining the decision, which was discussed at length during the most recent Richmond School Board meeting on July 28.

The discussion was sparked after Centers for Disease Control and Prevention's new guidelines were released July 27; the CDC "recommends universal indoor masking for all teachers, staff, students, and visitors to schools, regardless of vaccination status." 

But after failing to come to a consensus on the issue and not yet having an official recommendation from the state or Wayne County Health Department at the time, the school board decided to let Wright, whom Wayne County commissioners recently appointed to the Wayne County Board of Health, make the final decision.

"In an attempt to exercise some good ol Richmond common sense, effective Monday, Aug. 9, 2021, universal indoor masking for all teachers, staff, students, and visitors of preK-12 schools, regardless of vaccination status will be required," Wright wrote. "RCS officials will be monitoring data and relying upon guidance from the above health professionals, to determine when we can safely return to mask free learning environments."

The new guidelines, as always, are fluid and subject to change depending on new COVID-19 information and recommendations from the WCHD and CDC.

Unlike the 2020-21 school year, RCS at all grade levels will return to full-time in-person learning with no virtual learning option. Schools will operate at full capacity while practicing social distancing and other preventative measures, and there are different protocols for vaccinated and unvaccinated staff and students.

For the fully vaccinated, "No quarantine needed if identified as a close contact, if they remain asymptomatic, and proof of vaccination can be verified," according to RCS' updated return to in-person instruction plan. "Unvaccinated individuals who are identified as a close contact must quarantine. Asymptomatic close contacts may return on day 8 (with specific negative testing and precautions), day 11 (with precautions), or day 15 (without testing or precautions)."

The RCS policy also states that the schools will continue other preventative measures like disinfecting high-touch areas and laptops, and recommendations students and staff regularly monitor their symptoms and stay home if sick.

As of Friday morning, Northeastern Wayne, Western Wayne, Centerville Abington and Nettle Creek schools superintendents all said they will not require masks to begin the school year. Those districts will operate under their original return to in-person plans released in July, but are subject to change.

Seton Catholic Schools did not respond to an inquiry about their masking policies.

"Vaccination is currently the leading public health prevention strategy to end the COVID-19 pandemic. Masking, washing hands, and social distancing is a close second," Wright wrote. "RCS will continue to encourage our entire community to partner with us to provide protections for those that mean the most to us all, our students."
